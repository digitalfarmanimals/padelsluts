The thesis

A sport exploding — with no media home.

Premier Padel owns the elite tour. Playtomic owns booking. Clubs own courts. But the media layer — the YouTube-native home where the sport is watched, followed and lived — is wide open. We take it the way Kings League and Baller League took football: characters, drama, and a format built for the feed. Then we keep the audience on a platform they own a place in.

The platform · the durable asset

Watch it Sunday. Play it Tuesday.

The show makes padel must-watch. The platform lets every viewer play the exact game they saw on TV — The 20 — in mini-leagues with their mates, ranked nationally. We don't book courts or fight Playtomic. We own the format, the competition and the data that sits on top.

Play the format

The 20 isn't just the show's format — it's what you play. The show teaches the game; the platform is where the nation plays it. A loop nobody else has.

Join mini-leagues

Local, club and friend leagues running The 20 — standings, rounds, weekly drama. Recurring, social, sticky. The reason people come back every week.

Get rated · climb

Every league game feeds your rating and the national leaderboard — the official ranking of every player, and the data moat underneath it all.

An owned library that compounds every season.

Beyond the P&L, every season builds a permanent, owned asset. Funds value content libraries at 5-10× their annual licensing revenue — and ours grows every year at near-zero marginal cost, because the footage already exists.

The one rule that protects all of it: a library is only worth the rights you retain. Give territories or windows away to a broadcaster for early reach and you give the value away with them. We own the footage, the format and the brand outright — which is exactly why the IP must sit in a company we control, licensed into the venture, not handed up to a partner. The library value sits largely within the enterprise value above; this is the tangible, financeable asset underneath the P&L.
How it makes money · 8 streams

Eight ways to win.

1 · Sponsorship

Title + secondary + per-city. The biggest, earliest line — Kings League proves ~65-70% of revenue.

2 · Ad / content

YouTube + social at Tier-1 RPMs (~£2-4/1k views), scaling with a global audience.

3 · Live / tickets

Ticketed arena finals — Baller League sold out the O2 in season one.

4 · Merch

A tribe brand — crown, gold, "earned." High-margin, fan-driven.

5 · Mini-league subs

Play the game you watched on TV — The 20 — in leagues with mates. Recurring, sticky, software-margin.

6 · Club leagues (B2B)

Clubs run Kourt King mini-leagues for members on our platform. Playtomic proves the multiple (~€250M).

7 · Format licensing

License the show territory-by-territory. Capital-light, high-margin.

8 · Data & rating

The official rating + match data of the sport. The strategic crown jewel.

The roadmap

Britain first. Then the world.

Win locally, prove the model, export the format, scale the platform globally. Operate the high-upside markets; license the mature ones.

Year 1 · Now

Britain — prove it

Film the pilot, run the first city qualifiers, launch the platform (casting, rating, leaderboard, clips). Best unit economics on earth; build the UK audience. The raise funds this.

Year 2

Britain scale + USA entry

Full UK season, national final at an arena. Open the USA — biggest upside, English content travels, creator-native, demand outstrips courts. Platform goes global (anyone can play The 20, get rated).

Year 3

France & Germany

The fastest-growing near-Europe markets alongside the UK. Licensed shows begin. Platform compounds across territories; data moat deepens.

Years 4-5

License the rest · become the home

License into mature padel cultures (Spain, Italy, Sweden) and capital-rich Gulf markets. The platform is now the global home of padel media — the channel, the community, the rating, the data. £100m+ shape.

The £100m+ path

From a show to the infrastructure of a sport.

A show is valued on its audience (media multiples). A platform that owns the audience, the rating and the data of a global sport is valued like infrastructure (software/data multiples, on a global base). That gap is the £100m+ opportunity.

Own
the official rating & ranking of every padel player — the number everyone checks. PlaySight is already putting ratings into padel broadcasts; we own ours.
Own
the data layer — every game logged, every player tracked. The crown jewel an acquirer pays a premium for, beyond any P&L.
Own
the audience & culture — a young, padel-obsessed community a broadcaster, brand or data buyer can't otherwise reach.
Credible acquirers: a sports/media group (DAZN, a streamer) buying an owned young audience; a padel consolidator (Playtomic, a brand like Adidas/Babolat) buying the rating/data/community; or — later, carefully — a data/betting company for which the official rating is strategically essential. £100m+ is the earned outcome of owning the media layer first.
